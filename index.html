RC Car Project

ME 49601T - Iterative Product Design

Given an actual RC vehicle, I was assigned to work on a team of 4 students to re-engineer the platform of this vehicle. This included modeling all vehicle components, adding these and hardware to subsystems, and final assembly subsystems and hardware to virtually make your team’s engineered/designed solution. The analyses we were to consider included: topology optimization to guide initial and preliminary models, mass properties, FEA, CFD and motion analyses to guide the final size and shape of components, subsystems, and the final vehicle assembly. The design was to be fully parametric such that a simple change in one or more key parameters (i.e., scale factor, track width, vehicle height, wheelbase, etc.) would morph the entire vehicle.

Hammer Down!

ME 444 - Toy Design

The project was “Hammer Down!”, a Purdue spin on the age-old bell ringer carnival game. In the game, the player mashes the button as fast as they can for 10 seconds. Purdue Pete will raise his hammer a small amount for each time the button is pressed. After 10 seconds Pete will slam down his hammer and hit the lever at a speed dependent on how many times the button was pressed. This will launch the ball inside the ball guide and the screen will then display the score. The hammer movement is controlled by a stepper motor which drives the axle connected to Pete’s arms. The motor, speaker, button, and screen are controlled by 3 different Arduino’s as well as a motor controller.

Wind Turbine Project

ME 35401 - Machine Design Lab

The goal of this project is to design a speed reducing gear train for a wind turbine model, powered by a motor. This is done by designing a functional and efficient gear train that can reduce the speed of the motor while increasing the torque output. This will allow the motor to power the wind turbine model effectively, and generate the lowest turbine speed possible.

Aircraft Drag Coefficients

ME 30801 - Fluid Mechanics Lab

The objective of this open lab is to compare the drag coefficients of different military aircraft models at a 1:200 scale by measuring and analyzing aircraft drag force measurements in a wind tunnel using a dynamometer. The purpose of doing this is to determine which fighter jet is the most aerodynamic. The aim is to observe the dynamometer drag values at different velocities to determine a trend that can eventually be used in our conclusions at the end of the report. Another objective is to observe the military aircraft at different angles of attack and analyze the effects of the differing angles of attack on the coefficient of drag. Using the wind tunnel and the dy namometer, an understanding of linear variable differential transformer (LVDT) and how it operates is also gained.

Autonomous Robot

ME 375 - Measurement & Control Systems II Lab

For the final project, I built and programed a two-wheeled robot that completed ”line following” and ”herding” behaviors - moving in a controlled manner, following a line on the track by using a line following sensor, and then using an ultrasonic sensor to track, and stay a set distance from an object. The robot was constructed using a Skitter robot kit and controlled by a myRIO device.

Boomie Zoomie B

Purdue Space Program - Liquids

Boomie Zoomie B (BZB) is PSP Liquids’ second vehicle iteration, intended to improve on Boomie Zoomie (BZ) and win the FAR-MARS Launch Contest. Using a simulation-driven, optimized design, BZB features significant performance increases on top of the strong vehicle architecture provided by BZ. BZB featured a lightweight common bulkhead tank assembly, robust and redundant avionics design, and improved engine performance and cooling.
